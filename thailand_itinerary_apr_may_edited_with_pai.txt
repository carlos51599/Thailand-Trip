THAILAND ITINERARY (APRIL 10 – MAY 4)
24 DAYS – ANDAMAN ADVENTURE + SONGKRAN

----------------------------------------
OVERVIEW
This itinerary focuses on Thailand’s West Coast (Andaman Sea) during the tail end of the dry season, combined with Songkran (Thai New Year). Bases are chosen to minimise travel friction while maximising scenery and atmosphere.

----------------------------------------
PART 1: THE WATER FESTIVAL
APRIL 10 – 17

April 10–12: Bangkok
- Arrive in Bangkok
- Settle in, adjust to time zone
- Light sightseeing and food exploration

April 13–15: Chiang Mai (Songkran)
- Fly: Bangkok (BKK) → Chiang Mai (CNX)
- Songkran celebrations around the Old City moat
- Visit mountain temples (e.g. Doi Suthep)

April 16–17: Chiang Mai (Recovery)
- Rest days after Songkran
- Optional day trip to Sticky Waterfalls (Bua Tong)


April 16–17: Pai (Mountain Escape)
- Travel by moped or minivan: Chiang Mai → Pai (Route 1095)
- Scenic mountain road with viewpoints and forests
- Hot springs, Pai Canyon, and slow evenings
- Focus on rest and nature after Songkran

----------------------------------------
PART 2: THE WEST COAST GEMS
APRIL 18 – 28

April 18: Travel to Krabi
- Return from Pai to Chiang Mai early morning
- Fly: Chiang Mai (CNX) → Krabi (KBV)
- Base yourself in Ao Nang

April 19–21: Ao Nang & Phang Nga Bay
- Longtail boat or organised tour of Phang Nga Bay
- Limestone karsts, lagoons, sea caves
- Pro tip: Book a sunrise or early-morning tour to avoid crowds

April 22–25: Koh Phi Phi
- Ferry from Krabi to Koh Phi Phi
- Hike to Phi Phi Viewpoint
- Sunset boat trip to Maya Bay
- Expect crowds; early starts pay off

April 26–28: Railay Beach
- Boat access only from Ao Nang
- Dramatic limestone cliffs and beaches
- World-class rock climbing
- Excellent sunsets

----------------------------------------
PART 3: WILDCARD / FINAL STRETCH
APRIL 29 – MAY 4

April 29 – May 1: Khao Sok National Park
- Travel inland via Surat Thani or from Krabi
- Stay in a floating raft house on Cheow Lan Lake
- Jungle scenery, limestone peaks, total calm

May 2–4: Bangkok
- Fly back to Bangkok
- Final shopping, food, and departure

----------------------------------------
WHY THIS ROUTE WORKS
- Avoids overloading the trip with extra countries
- Krabi limestone cliffs and Khao Sok rival Philippine scenery
- Less travel stress, more time in standout locations

----------------------------------------
THINGS TO KNOW (APRIL / MAY)

Maya Bay:
- Usually open in April
- Extremely busy after mid-morning
- Early-bird tours (around 6:30 AM) are strongly recommended

Heat:
- Late April sun is intense
- Phang Nga Bay reflects heat heavily
- Hat, sunscreen, and hydration are essential

Boat Safety:
- Seas can become choppy during monsoon transition
- If prone to seasickness, choose larger ferries over speedboats
